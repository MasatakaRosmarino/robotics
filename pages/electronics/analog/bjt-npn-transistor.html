Experiment 1:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 1.25V AA recargeable batteries X4
  • AA battery holder
  • 1M Ω resistor X1
  • 220 Ω resistor X1
  • red LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • 2N2222A BJT transistor in a TO-92 case
  • Multimeter

Preparation

Before setting up the circuit, on the multimeter measure the hFE of the transistor

NOTE: this value is subject to change as transistors are not physically identical.

setup

drawing here

Observation

LED and 220 Ω resistors connected between the collector and VIN
Values measured with multimeters are:
  • hFE: 183
  • Battery pack voltage: 5.028 V
  • Resistor at the base: 1.003 M Ω
  • Resistor at the collector: 218.3 Ω
  • Voltages across:
    • resistor at the base: 4.387 V
    • resistor at the collector: 0.209V
    • LED: 1.75V
    • base and emitter: 0.622V
    • base and collector: 2.415 V
    • collector and emitter: 3.055V
    • transistor+LED+resistor: 5.023V
  • Currents through:
    • base: 4.3 uA
    • collector: 0.96 mA
    • emitter: 0.96 mA
LED and 220 Ω resistors connected between the emitter and the ground
Measured values are:
  • hFE: 183
  • Power supply voltage: 5.27 V
  • Resistor at the base: 1.003 M Ω
  • Resistor at the collector: 465 Ω
  • Voltages across:
    • resistor at the base: 2.496 V
    • resistor at the emitter: 0.116 V
    • LED: 1.73 V
    • base and emitter: 0.611 V
    • base and collector: 2.533 V
    • collector and emitter: 3.167 V
    • transistor+LED+resistor: 5.027 V
  • Currents through:
    • base: 1.4 uA
    • collector: 0.53 mA
    • emitter: 0.53 mA

NOTE: If the load (LED + 220 Ω resistor) is placed between the emitter and the ground, the current through the base and the load is roughly half.

IE / IB = 0.96 / 0.0043 = ~223
