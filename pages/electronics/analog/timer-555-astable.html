Experiment 1:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 9V battery
  • 555 chip
  • Optional: 9V battery connector
  • 1000 Ω resistor
  • 10K Ω resistors X2
  • Jumper cables X6
  • green or yellow LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • 4.7 uF electrolytic capacitor

Preparation

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    • the 555 chip
    • the capacitor connecting pin 1 and 2 of the 555 chip
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 1 of the chip to ground
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 2 to pin 6
    • the 1000 Ω resistor connecting pin 3 of the chip to a neutral rail of the breadboard
    • the positive pin of the LED connecting the loose end of the 1000 Ω resistor, and the negative lead connecting the ground
    • the red LED with the longer pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 4 to pin 8, or pin 4 to the positive end of the source
    • a 10K Ω resistor connecting pin 6 to 7
    • a 10K Ω resistor connecting pin 7 to the positive end of the source
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 8 to the positive end of the source
    • the battery negative lead to the ground
    • the battery positive lead to the positive rail
  2. Optional: connect the battery connector to the 9V battery
  3. touch the free jumper wires ends to the battery poles or the battery connector ends

Observation

The LED blinks at a regular interval

Experiment 3:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 9V battery
  • 555 chip
  • Optional: 9V battery connector
  • 1000 Ω resistor
  • 10K Ω resistors
  • Jumper cables X6
  • 8 Ω 0.25W speaker
  • 1 uF electrolytic capacitor
  • 100 uF electrolytic capacitor

Setup

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    • the 555 chip
    • the 1 uF capacitor connecting pin 1 and 2 of the 555 chip
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 1 of the chip to ground
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 2 to pin 6
    • the 100 uF capacitor with the positive lead connecting pin 3 of the chip, and the negative into a neutral hole
    • The speaker with the positive pin connecting the negative end of the 100 uF capacitor, and the negative lead connecting to the ground
    • the red LED with the longer pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 4 to pin 8, or the rail connecting the positive end of the battery
    • a 10K Ω resistor connecting pin 6 to 7
    • a 1000 Ω resistor connecting pin 7 to pin 8 or to the positive rail
    • a jumper wire connecting pin 8 to the positive end of the source
    • The negative lead of the battery to the negative rail
    • The positive lead of the battery to the positive rail
  2. Optional: connect the battery connector to the 9V battery
  3. touch the free jumper wires ends to the battery poles or the battery connector ends

Observation

The LED blinks at a regular interval
