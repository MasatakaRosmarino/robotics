Experiment 1:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 1.25V AA recargeable batteries X4
  • AA battery holder
  • 1M Ω resistor X1
  • 220 Ω resistor X1
  • red LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • 2N2222A BJT transistor in a TO-92 case
  • Multimeter

Preparation

Before setting up the circuit, on the multimeter measure the hFE of the transistor

NOTE: this value is subject to change as transistors are not physically identical.

setup

drawing here

Observation

LED and 220 Ω resistors connected between the emitter and VIN
Values measured with multimeters are:
  • hFE: 171
  • Battery pack voltage: 5.06 V
  • Resistor at the base: 1.004 M Ω
  • Resistor at the collector: 217 Ω
  • Voltages across:
    • resistor at the base: 2.55 V
    • resistor at the emitter: 0.105 V
    • LED: 1.69 V
    • base and emitter: 0.61 V
    • base and collector: 2.54 V
    • collector and emitter: 3.16 V
    • transistor+LED+resistor: 4.99 V
  • Currents through:
    • base: 2.5 uA
    • collector: 0.47 mA
    • emitter: 0.47 mA
LED and 220 Ω resistors connected between the collector and the ground
Measured values are:
  • hFE: 171
  • Power supply voltage: 5.06 V
  • Resistor at the base: 1.004 M Ω
  • Resistor at the collector: 217 Ω
  • Voltages across:
    • resistor at the base: 4.35 V
    • resistor at the collector: 0.17 V
    • LED: 1.71 V
    • base and emitter: 0.63 V
    • base and collector: 2.4 V
    • collector and emitter: 3.04 V
    • transistor+LED+resistor: 5.006 V
  • Currents through:
    • base: 4.2 uA
    • collector: 0.79 mA
    • emitter: 0.81 mA

NOTE: If the load (LED + 220 Ω resistor) is placed between the emitter and the ground, the current through the base and the load is roughly half.
