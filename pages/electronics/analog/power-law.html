Law of Power

By knowing the voltage applied to the components and the current they draw, it is possible to calculate the power P.

Power is a value that helps adjust both V and I so that both V and I are below the limits the components can tolerate preventing them to burn.

Power Formula

P = V x I

r

The resistors used are rated 1/4 (0.25) W. If 9V are applied to them, in order to stay at 0.25 W they must follow this relation:

0.25 = 9 x I

I = 0.25 / 9 = 0.28 A

Here 0.28 A is the maximum current allowed through the resistor. If more current flows through it, it may be damaged due to overheating.
