ohm's law

Experiment 1:

Equipment needed

  • 1.5V AAA battery
  • 1.5V AA battery
  • Single AA battery holder, or simply adhesive tape
  • Jumper cables X4
  • 1.5V light bulb
  • red LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • Digital multimeter
Setup
  1. connect the jumper cables to the 1.5V AAA battery using the tape
  2. touch the light bulb to the other ends of the cables
    note: it doesn't matter which edge you touch
Observation
  1. connect the jumper cables to the 1.5V AAA battery using the tape
  2. touch the longer pin of the LED to the wire connecting the positive terminal of the battery
  3. touch the shorter pin of the LED to the wire connecting the negative terminal of the battery
    notes:
    • the LED won't turn on, but if plugged to a 1.5 AA battery it does turn on
    • if you had a AAA 2 battery holder the LED does turn on, as the source votage goes from 1.5V to 3V
Observation
  1. tape each LED connections to the related wire and make sure the light goes on
  2. connect the probes to the multimeter
  3. on the multimeter, set the knob arrow to 20V
  4. with the red probe touch the positive terminal of the battery (marked with a + sign)
  5. with the black probe touch the negative terminal of the battery (marked with a - sign)
  6. read the value
  7. with the multimeter probes touch the positive and negative connections of the LED
  8. read the value

note: Measurements show slightly different values from the ones declared.

Idea: here I could measure the voltage from the battery, then measure the resistance of the light bulb and the wires, sum them and apply Ohm's law

Experiment 2:

Equipment needed

  • Resistors
    • 100 Ω, 1/4W
    • 620 Ω, 1/4W
    • 1K Ω, 1/4W
    • 10K Ω, 1/4W
  • Jumper cables X 4
  • red LED (3mm or 5mm)
  • 9V battery
  • push button switch
  • Breadboard
  • digital multimeter
Observation
  1. connect the probes to the multimeter
  2. set the knob indicator to 200 (Ω section)
  3. touch the 100 Ω resistor edges to the probe pins
    note: it doesn't matter which edge you touch
  4. set the indicator to 2k (Ω section)
  5. touch the 620 Ω resistor edges with the probes
  6. touch the 1K Ω resistor edges with the probes
  7. set the knob indicator to 20k (Ω section)
  8. touch the 10K Ω resistor edges with the probe pins

note: Measurements show slightly different values from the calculations on paper.

Ohm's law

V = R x I

  • V = voltage
  • R = resistance
  • I = current

Experiment 3:

Procedure

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    • one jumper wire
    • the 620 Ω resistor
    • the LED with one pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    • the push button switch in line with the free pin of the LED
    • the other jumper wire with one end in line with the LED pin
  2. touch the battery poles to the free ends of the jumper wires
    NOTE: if the LED doesn't light, touch the opposite poles of the battery

important

all electronic components have maximum voltage and current ratings.

Before their use, it is necessary to read carefully the related datasheet.
