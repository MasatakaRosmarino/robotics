Experiment 2:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 9V battery
  • 9V battery connector
  • 470 Ω resistor
  • 1 KΩ potentiometer
  • red LED (3 or 5 mm)
  • Digital multimeter

Preparation

drawing here...

Observation

with the potentiometer set to its maximum resistance, measured values are:

  • the value of the 470 Ω resistor is actually 467.2 Ω
  • voltage across the battery: 8.72 V;
  • voltage across the middle and right potentiometer: 4.7 V;
  • voltage across the 470 Ω resistor: 2.03 V
  • voltage across the LED: 1.85 V
  • current through the circuit: 4.3 mA

with the potentiometer set to 760 Ω, measured values are:

  • voltage across the middle and right potentiometer: 4.13 V;
  • voltage across the 470 Ω resistor: 2.57 V
  • voltage across the LED: 1.87 V
  • current through the circuit: 5.46 mA

with the potentiometer set to 507 Ω, measured values are:

  • voltage across the middle and right potentiometer: 3.41 V;
  • voltage across the 470 Ω resistor: 3.22 V
  • voltage across the LED: 1.88 V
  • current through the circuit: 6.85 mA

with the potentiometer set to 306 Ω, measured values are:

  • voltage across the middle and right potentiometer: 2.57 V;
  • voltage across the 470 Ω resistor: 3.98 V
  • voltage across the LED: 1.89 V
  • current through the circuit: 8.6 mA

with the potentiometer set to its minimum resistance (5 Ω), measured values are:

  • voltage across the middle and right potentiometer: 0.055 V;
  • voltage across the 470 Ω resistor: 6.2 V
  • voltage across the LED: 1.91 V
  • current through the circuit: 13.73 mA
