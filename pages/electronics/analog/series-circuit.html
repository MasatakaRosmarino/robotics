Experiment 1:

Procedure

Retake experiment 3 from Ohm's law section and do the following:

  1. Measure the voltage across the 620 Ω resistor and the LED
  2. Measure the voltage across the battery
  3. Measure the current flowing through all connections in the circuit

Observation

  1. The sum total of the voltage across all components is equal to the voltage released from the battery
  2. The current passing through all components has the same value

Experiment 2:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 9V battery
  • Optional: 9V battery connector
  • 620 Ω resistor
  • 470 Ω resistor
  • Jumper cables X4
  • red LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • green LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • Digital multimeter

Preparation

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    1. a jumper wire
    2. the 620 Ω resistor
    3. the red LED with one pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    4. the green LED with one pin in line with one of the pins of the LED previously placed
    5. a jumper wire with one end in line with the second LED pin
  2. Optional: connect the battery connector to the 9V battery
  3. touch the free jumper wires ends to the battery poles or the battery connector ends
    NOTE: if the LED doesn't light, touch the opposite poles of the battery
  4. With the multimeter measure the voltage across:
    • The resistor
    • The red LED
    • the green LED
  5. measure the current through all the components in the circuit
  6. Replace the 620 Ω resistor with the 470 Ω resistor and measure:
    • the voltage across all components in the circuit
    • the current through the components in the circuit
Observation

Notice the 2 constant factors:

  • the sum total of the voltages across all the components is equal to the voltage across the battery.
  • the current is constant through all the circuit

Experiment 3:

Procedure

Swap the order of the components in the following order:

  1. Retake experiment 2 and wap the order of the components in the following order:
    • The green LED in place of the 620 Ω or the 470 Ω resistor
    • The red LED in place of the green LED
    • The 620 Ω or the 470 Ω resistor

Observation

  1. The sum total of the voltage across all components is still equal to the voltage released from the battery
  2. The current passing through all components still has the same value despite changing their position
