Experiment 1:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 9V battery
  • Optional: 9V battery connector
  • 620 Ω resistor
  • 470 Ω resistor
  • Jumper cables X4
  • red LED (3mm or 5 mm) X2
  • Digital multimeter

Preparation

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    • a jumper wire
    • the 620 Ω resistor
    • the first red LED with one pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    • the second red LED with both pins in the same lines as the LED previously placed
    • a jumper wire with one end in the same line as the pins of the previously placed LEDs
  2. Optional: connect the battery connector to the 9V battery
  3. touch the free jumper wires ends with the battery poles or the battery connector ends
  4. With the multimeter measure the voltage across:
    1. The 620 Ω resistor
    2. The first red LED
    3. The second red LED
  5. measure the current through the the components
  6. Replace the 620 Ω resistor with the 470 Ω resistor and measure:
    • the voltage across:
      1. The 470 Ω resistor
      2. The first red LED
      3. The second red LED
    • the current through the components
Observation
  • the voltages across both LEDs are the same
  • The voltage of the battery is equal to the sum of the voltage across the resistor and the voltage across either the red or green LED (not the sum total of the voltages across the 2 LEDs)
  • the current through the 620 Ω or 470 Ω resistor is equal to the sum of the currents through the red and green LEDs
