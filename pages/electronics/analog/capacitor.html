capacitors

Experiment 1:

Equipment needed

  • Breadboard
  • 9V battery
  • Optional: 9V battery connector
  • 1000 Ω resistor
  • Jumper cables X4
  • red LED (3mm or 5 mm)
  • 1000 uF electrolytic capacitor

Preparation

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    • a jumper wire
    • the 1000 Ω resistor
    • the red LED with the longer pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    • the 1000 uF capacitor with the longer pin in line with the shorter pin of the previously placed LED
    • a jumper wire with one end in line with the second LED pin
  2. Optional: connect the battery connector to the 9V battery
  3. touch the free jumper wires ends to the battery poles or the battery connector ends

Observation

  1. after a few seconds the LED starts dimming until it goes off
  2. if disconnecting the battery and connecting the positive end to the negative, the LED starts glowing until it goes off again

Experiment 2:

Equipment needed

same components as from experiment 1

Preparation

  1. on the Breadboard place:
    • a jumper wire
    • the 1000 Ω resistor
    • the red LED with the longer pin in line with one of the pins of the resistor previously placed
    • the 1000 uF capacitor with both pins in line with the LED pins previously placed
    • a jumper wire with one end in line with the second LED pin
  2. Optional: connect the battery connector to the 9V battery
  3. touch the free jumper wires ends to the battery poles or the battery connector ends

Observation

  1. when closing the circuit the LED starts glowing gradually until its fully lit
  2. when opening the circuit the LED dims gradually until it goes off completely
