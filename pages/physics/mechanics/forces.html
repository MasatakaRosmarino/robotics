Forces and vectors

Definition

Force is a pushing or pulling action causing a body with a certain mass m to gain acceleration a.

The mathematical relationtip between mass and acceleration is:

F = m a

Forces are expressed as vectors, which are characterized by:

  • Magnitude
  • Direction
  • Point of application

In order to analyze the force exerted on the body, we can proceed in the following way:

  • consider the body as a particle (no well-defined size)
  • consider the angle adjacent to the x or y axis

The magnitude of the force along the x axis is:

Fx = F cos θ

The magnitude of the force along the y axis is:

Fy = F sin θ

NOTE: every force exerted on a body can be plotted onto a cartesian plane relative to an x and y axis.
The components along the x and y axes outline a right triangle.
